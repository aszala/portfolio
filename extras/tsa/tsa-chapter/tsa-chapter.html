The TSA Motto and Creed
"Learning to lead in a technical world"

Importance to Us

The TSA motto and creed are very important to our chapter. Our chapter focuses on creating students who are not only proficient in technology, but who also are leaders - whether in creating student projects or bringing their ideas to life. Finally, our students know the importance of using technology to benefit society and our school, and try their best to use their skills to help out in the community.

Chapter History
The history of our chapter
The Panther Creek Chapter was founded in 2011 by Mr. William R. Hollar, a Sci-Vis Teacher. At the time of its conception, it consisted only of a Robotics team. Over the next few years, the TSA program gained support as more and more students showed interest in supporting the team. This year we have 76 registered participants, and all over the board our chapter has shown growth in population, performance, awards and attendance at nationals.

Community Service Projects
In addition to competing in the highest levels of competition, PCTSA also serves the community. We participate in fundraisers, volunteer work, and maintains in active presence in community affairs.

PCTSA is now working to educate and tutor Middle School chapters such as the Lufkin Road Middle School Chapter. Not only does this ensure education for the middle school TSA members, but it also allows PCTSA to introduce middle schoolers to the world of TSA and the possibilities that await.

Panther Creek Chapter Team at an expo showcasing TSA to incoming students

Our chapter has volunteered at Kramden Institute in Durham, NC. While at Kramden Institute our members have helped in the process of cleaning and restoring computers so that they may be used buy kids that currently are unable to get a computer of their own.

National Competitions
Every year we attend the National Conference and place in many events

TSA National Conference 2017

Date: June 21-25, 2017

Location: Rosen Shingle Creek, Orlando, Florida
Theme: "Defining Your Future"

With our abundance of members that attend the National Conference each year our chapter likes to celebrate our achievements. During the 2016-2017 National Conference in Orlando, Florida, we, as a chapter, went to Epcot in Disney for a day to relax and celebrate to achievement of attending the National Conference.

Nationals Training
  • Professional Leadership Training at the national TSA conference
  • On-site training was offered at the 2018 national TSA conference in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • These free sessions, open on a first-come, first serve basis, were offered to middle school and high school students, and advisors.
  • Sessions were tailored to each audience, but they will be consistent in terms of the integration of The Student Leadership Challenge - Five Practices for Becoming an Exemplary Leader in TSA competitions and the LEAP Legacy Chapter program.


TSA National Conference 2018

Date: June 22-26, 2018

Location: Georgia World Congress Center, Atlanta
Theme: "A Celebration of Success"

In 2018, 25 members of our chapter were fortunate enough to compete at TSA Nationals in Atlanta, Georgia. Our chapter not only was competitive in our events, but we were able to enjoy the beautiful city and its many tourist attractions - notably the CNN Building and the Puppet Museum. We had 7 teams qualify as national semi-finalist and 2 teams as finalist, which gave our chapter members a learning experience that they would never forget.
